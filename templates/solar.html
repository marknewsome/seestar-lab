Tips & Troubleshooting
Re-rendering is fast The slow part (Pass 1 — disk detection) is cached in solar_alignment.json. Changing any parameter and clicking Re-render only re-runs Pass 2 & 3, usually a matter of seconds. Use it freely to tune the look.
Trees & cloud occlusion Frames where trees or clouds block the disk are usually rejected automatically — the radius-drift check discards frames where HoughCircles returns a wrong disk size, and the sharpness check discards frames where the limb is blurred by partial obstruction. If blocked frames still appear in your timelapse, raise Min Quality and re-render.
Min quality tuning The default of 5 is permissive. On a session with good seeing you can raise it to 20–50 to keep only the sharpest frames. On a hazy or high-altitude session you may need to lower it if too many frames are being rejected. Check the render log — it reports how many frames were accepted vs. rejected per video.
Stabilise (wind jitter) Applies a Gaussian rolling smooth to the detected disk-center positions before normalisation. Try 7 for a light breeze, up to 15 for gustier conditions. Leave at 0 on calm days — it has no effect and adds no cost.
Speedup & pacing At the default 1800×, 30 real minutes of solar session becomes 1 second of timelapse. Halve it to 900 for slower, more dramatic-feeling movement; double it to 3600 for a very compressed summary of a long session.
